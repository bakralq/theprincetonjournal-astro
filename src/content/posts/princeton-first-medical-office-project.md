## What Was Announced

On **March 4, 2026**, the City of Princeton announced plans for what it says will be the city’s **first medical office development** in **Princeton Professional Park**.

## What Is Being Planned

According to the city, **Harrod Healthcare Real Estate** plans to build about **25,000 square feet** of medical office space across **2 buildings** on about **2.7 acres**.

## Where It Would Go

The site is described as being **just south of Children’s Lighthouse along S. Beauchamp Boulevard, north of Myrick Lane**.

## Who Owns the Land

The city also says the land is being purchased from the **Princeton Economic Development Corporation, or PEDC**.

## What Could Go Inside

The project is expected to include **8 to 12 medical suites** and is meant to attract **independent physicians, physician groups, and other healthcare-related services**.

That basically means multiple healthcare providers could lease space and serve patients in one location.

## Developer Background

The city also says **Harrod Healthcare Real Estate** has previously developed **six outpatient medical facilities in Collin County**, along with other medical office buildings and ambulatory surgery centers across the Southeast.

## Timeline

As of the city’s announcement, the project is expected to be delivered by the **end of 2026**.

## Quick Version

Princeton says a private developer plans to build the city’s first medical office project near **Beauchamp and Myrick**.

It would include **2 buildings**, about **25,000 square feet** of space, and **8 to 12 suites** for healthcare providers.
