## What Residents Need to Know Right Now

**Saturday, January 24, 2026**

## Active Alerts

• **Winter Storm Warning:** in effect until **12:00 PM Sunday, January 25**  
• **Extreme Cold Warning:** from **6:00 PM Saturday, January 24** to **9:00 AM Tuesday, January 27**

## What to Expect

• Periods of **sleet and freezing rain**, with some snow and sleet mixed in. Roads and bridges will be hazardous, especially overnight and early in the morning.

• Local streets can ice over faster and stay icy longer than major highways, especially **shaded areas, bridges, and rural connectors**.

• Dangerous cold is expected behind the storm. High temperatures are forecast to stay **below freezing through Monday, January 26**, with subfreezing nights continuing beyond that.

• **Black ice risk increases after precipitation ends**, especially overnight as temperatures continue dropping.

## What This Means for You

• Avoid unnecessary travel. If you must drive, slow down and assume bridges and overpasses are icy even if they appear clear.

• Be prepared for possible delays, limited services, or short-term disruptions if conditions worsen.

## Protect Pipes

• Wrap exposed outdoor and garage pipes  
• Open cabinet doors on exterior walls so warm air can circulate  
• If you drip faucets, a pencil-thin stream is enough

Garage plumbing and plumbing along exterior walls are among the most common freeze failure points.

## Bring Pets Indoors

Pets should be brought indoors during the coldest conditions.

## Check on Neighbors

Check on:

• elderly neighbors  
• people living alone  
• anyone without reliable heat  

This is especially important overnight and early in the morning.

## Power and Water Considerations

• Extended subfreezing temperatures increase the chance of localized power interruptions, especially during peak overnight demand.

• Water line breaks often happen during the thaw, not only during the freeze, so precautions should remain in place even after roads improve.

## City Resources

### Warming Shelter

**Steve and Judy Deffibaugh Community Center**  
416 N. 4th Street

• Opened at **7:00 AM Saturday, January 24**  
• Will remain open while freezing conditions persist  
• Available to anyone without reliable heat  

Neighbors helping neighbors may be especially important for residents without transportation.

Official city information is available through the **City of Princeton’s 2026 Winter Storm page** and other official city channels.

## Bottom Line

This is not a panic situation, but it is a serious ice and cold risk.

Some of the most dangerous conditions can arrive **after the precipitation stops**.

Even a small amount of ice can cause crashes, power outages, and pipe damage. A little preparation now can prevent bigger problems later.

Follow **The Princeton Journal** and official **City of Princeton** channels for verified updates.
