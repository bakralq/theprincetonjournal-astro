## Summary

The Texas Department of Transportation will hold a **virtual public meeting with an in-person open house option on Tuesday, December 9, 2025, from 5 to 7 p.m.** to present plans to widen **FM 982 from US 380 to FM 546**.

**In-person open house**  
Longhorn 2020 Event Center  
3670 FM 982  
Princeton, Texas 75407

**Virtual meeting**  
A video presentation will be posted at **5 p.m. on TxDOT’s Keep It Moving Dallas website.**

The proposal would:

• Expand FM 982 to a **four-lane divided roadway**  
• Add **10-foot shared-use paths** along most of the corridor  
• Install **curb-and-gutter drainage**  
• Require **additional right-of-way**, typically around **120 feet wide**  
• Potentially **displace one residential structure**, with relocation assistance available

This project could affect **traffic patterns, land values, development, safety, and long-term mobility across Princeton**.

---

## What’s Being Proposed

### Project Scope

• Expansion of FM 982 **from US 380 to FM 546**  
• **Four-lane divided roadway design**  
• **10-foot shared-use paths on both sides** along most of the corridor  
• **6-foot sidewalk** on the southbound side between US 380 and CR 400 (Myrick Lane)/FM 452 before transitioning into a shared-use path  
• **Curb-and-gutter drainage system**  
• **Typical right-of-way width of about 120 feet**, requiring land acquisition  
• **One residential structure may be displaced**, with relocation assistance available

## Why This Matters for Princeton

This proposal goes beyond a simple road widening project. It touches **growth management, infrastructure capacity, walkability, housing patterns, and long-term city planning**.

### 1. Traffic Relief and Commuting

FM 982 connects several neighborhoods to US 380. Widening the corridor could reduce congestion during school, work, and weekend peak traffic periods while improving cross-city mobility.

### 2. Development and Property Impact

Major roadway expansions often trigger additional development activity, including:

• New commercial sites  
• New residential subdivisions  
• Changes in property values and tax assessments

These changes can influence **future planning decisions, utility capacity, and school enrollment pressures**.

### 3. Walkability and Multi-Use Paths

Shared-use paths are intended to support **walking, running, and cycling**. Their effectiveness will depend on whether the paths connect meaningfully to neighborhoods, schools, parks, and commercial areas.

### 4. Land Acquisition and Displacement

Additional right-of-way will be required along parts of the corridor. At least **one residential structure may be affected**, and property owners along FM 982 should closely review:

• Right-of-way maps  
• Notification letters  
• Compensation and relocation procedures

### 5. Long-Term Timeline

The project is expected to unfold over multiple years.

Current planning timelines indicate:

• **Construction plans targeted for 2027**  
• **Right-of-way acquisition after final plans are completed**  
• **Construction anticipated to begin in late 2029 or early 2030**

## Big-Picture Meaning

Princeton is gradually shifting from rural road layouts toward **urban-scale transportation infrastructure**.

If managed carefully, the FM 982 expansion could:

• Reduce congestion across the city  
• Improve safety for drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians  
• Support coordinated long-term growth

If managed poorly, it could:

• Place pressure on utilities and schools  
• Encourage scattered development patterns  
• Displace residents without sufficient transparency or compensation

This corridor will likely play a major role in shaping **how Princeton grows during the next decade**.
