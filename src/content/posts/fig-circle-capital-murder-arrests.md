The case has re-entered public view following arrests made on **January 8, 2026**, though the shooting itself took place months earlier, in **July 2025**.

Below is a factual summary of what is publicly known as of **January 20, 2026**.

## The Confirmed Incident

On **July 9, 2025**, deputies with the **Collin County Sheriff’s Office** responded to a reported shooting in the **9100 block of Fig Circle**, south of Princeton, at approximately **8:16 PM**.

According to law enforcement, a caller reported hearing gunfire and then seeing a man lying motionless. Deputies arrived and found the victim suffering from multiple gunshot wounds. He was transported by EMS to a hospital, where he was later pronounced dead.

Local media, including **WFAA**, reported at the time that a man died after being shot multiple times in a shooting just south of Princeton.

## The Victim

Subsequent reporting, citing an official Sheriff’s Office update, identifies the victim as:

**Ociel Bautista Perez, 43**

## The January 8 Arrests

According to reporting that quotes the **Collin County Sheriff’s Office**, three individuals were arrested on **January 8, 2026** in connection with this homicide and booked into the **Collin County Detention Facility** on a capital charge.

The individuals reported arrested are:

• **Jerson Dagoberto Fuentes Dubon, 29**  
• **Mauricio Coto Ramirez, 37**  
• **Patricia Guadalupe Flores Bermudez, 49**

### Reported residences

• Fuentes Dubon and Coto Ramirez: **Dallas**  
• Flores Bermudez: **McKinney**

## The Charge: Capital Murder With Remuneration

All three defendants are reported as charged with **Capital Murder with Remuneration**.

In plain language, under Texas law, a murder may be charged as capital murder when it is committed for payment, promise of payment, or when someone hires or pays another person to carry out the killing.

This is often described as a **murder-for-hire-type charge**, though the specific alleged roles of each defendant have not yet been publicly detailed in this case.
