**Update (Oct. 27):** Tonight’s City Council meeting was canceled, so no action was taken on any agenda items.

One item that had been scheduled for consideration was **Resolution No. 2025-10-27-R07**, which would allocate **up to $4,350,115** from the voter-approved **November 2023 $109.1 million Parks & Recreation Bond**.

The funding would support construction of **three multi-purpose synthetic turf fields** at **J.M. Caldwell Sr. Community Park**.

## Project Overview

The proposal would replace three existing grass multi-use fields with synthetic turf surfaces along with related site improvements.

According to city documents, the project is intended to improve:

• Field durability  
• Drainage performance  
• Year-round play availability

Synthetic turf fields typically allow significantly more annual usage compared with natural grass fields, particularly during wet weather periods.

## Vendor

The resolution identifies **Symmetry Sports, LLC** as the project vendor.

## Project Authorization Process

If approved, the resolution would authorize the **City Manager** to execute all required project documents.

According to the agenda materials, the contract has been reviewed by:

• City staff  
• City legal counsel  
• Princeton Parks & Recreation Department

A memorandum recommending the project was prepared by **Parks & Recreation Director Chase Bryant** and dated **October 14, 2025**.

## What Happens Next

Because the **October 27 City Council meeting was canceled**, the item was not considered.

The proposed turf field project is expected to return on a future City Council agenda for formal review and possible approval.
