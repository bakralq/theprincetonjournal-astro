## The case, in one paragraph

On Oct. 9, 2025, a Collin County jury convicted Princeton resident **David Rojas Sanchez, 66**, of two counts of indecency with a child by sexual contact involving an 11-year-old. The life sentence was publicized by the district attorney’s office on Oct. 14, 2025.

Jurors also heard that Sanchez was previously convicted in **2008 in McLennan County** of indecency with a child by sexual contact. He served eight years, was released in 2013, and later moved to Princeton. Jurors were also told about a **1986 attempted voluntary manslaughter conviction** and **two pending Milam County stalking charges involving children** before imposing a life sentence, the statutory maximum within the enhanced punishment range.

## Why the punishment could be life

Indecency with a child by sexual contact is ordinarily a **second degree felony** carrying a punishment range of **2 to 20 years** under **Texas Penal Code §21.11**.

However, under **Penal Code §12.42**, prior felony convictions can increase the punishment range.

• One prior non state jail felony allows the state to punish a second degree felony as a **first degree felony (5 to 99 years or life)**.  
• Two sequential prior felonies place a defendant in the **habitual offender range of 25 to 99 years or life**.

Those enhancement paths explain how a jury could reach a **life sentence in a repeat offender scenario**.

## Scale: what’s happening in Princeton

Raw counts of registered offenders often rise when a city’s population grows quickly.

Princeton was the **fastest growing city in the United States**, expanding about **30.6 percent between 2023 and 2024**, according to the U.S. Census Bureau.

A public, non official aggregator currently shows **approximately 87 registered offenders within Princeton city limits**. That number fluctuates over time.

Residents should verify current information using official tools such as:

• Princeton Police Department guidance  
• Collin County OffenderWatch  
• Texas Department of Public Safety sex offender registry

## Scale: what’s happening in Texas

By total number of registrants, **Texas has the largest sex offender registry in the United States**, with roughly **75,700 registrants**, representing about **10 percent of the national total** based on an August 2024 compilation.

These figures should be treated as a **moving snapshot**, since registry totals change as individuals are added or removed.

## Risk, repeat offending, and what research shows

Sexual recidivism varies depending on the population studied and the length of the follow up period.

Research from the **Bureau of Justice Statistics (BJS)** found:

• **5.3 percent rearrest** and **3.5 percent reconviction** for a sex offense within three years among 9,691 sex offenders released in 1994.  
• **7.7 percent arrested for rape or sexual assault within nine years** among those released for rape or sexual assault in 2005.

Rearrest rates for **any offense** are significantly higher, and outcomes vary depending on age, offense type, and follow up timeframes.

## Underreporting is real

Many cases of child sexual abuse are never reported.

The **Children’s Advocacy Centers of Texas** summarize existing research as showing that **about three out of five children never disclose sexual abuse**, based on peer reviewed literature.

Both realities can exist at the same time:

• Short horizon recidivism metrics may appear lower than expected.  
• True victimization rates may be undercounted due to underreporting.

## What this means for Princeton right now

• **Repeat case, maximum outcome.** Collin County juries can and do use enhancement law to reach life sentences in repeat offender cases.  

• **Growth changes exposure by volume.** With 30.6 percent year over year population growth, raw registry numbers will naturally increase even if per capita risk remains stable.  

• **Policy mindset: precision over panic.** Underreporting warrants vigilance, but risk metrics depend heavily on methodology and timeframe. Verified registries and prevention education remain more reliable tools than rumor or speculation.
