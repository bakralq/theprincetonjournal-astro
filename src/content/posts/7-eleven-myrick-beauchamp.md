A new gas station and convenience store, using **7-Eleven-branded plans**, is proposed for the **northwest corner of Myrick Lane and S. Beauchamp Boulevard**.

The gas-station zoning was approved last fall after public hearings and is now returning for a technical update tied to land boundaries.

Here is what is happening.

## What Is Being Built

City-filed architectural plans show this is a full **7-Eleven prototype store** labeled:

**7-Eleven #42779**  
**Beauchamp & Myrick**  
**Princeton, Texas**

The project includes:

• a **4,816-square-foot convenience store**  
• **10 gas pumps** under a large fuel canopy  
• **diesel, regular, mid-grade, and premium gasoline**  
• **35 parking spaces**  
• trash, air, and vacuum stations  
• monument and ground signs  
• brick and stone exterior materials  
• landscaping along both streets

This is a full-size modern gas station designed for a high-traffic intersection.

While the zoning case itself does not name a tenant, the architectural plans filed with the city are branded for **7-Eleven (#42779)**.

## Why This Is Back on the Agenda

City Council already approved a gas station at this location on **November 10, 2025**, under **Ordinance ORD 2025-11-10-06-SUP**.

What is being considered now is **not whether a gas station is allowed**.

That use has already been approved.

The developer, **Vaquero Ventures**, is now requesting to expand the approved site from:

**1.354 acres → 1.624 acres**

That added **0.27 acres** is intended to align the approved SUP boundary with the actual site layout so that a building permit can be legally issued.

The land is part of a larger tract known as **Princeton Business Park Block A Lot 2**, so the city also requires a **replat** to create a new **1.624-acre lot** specifically for the 7-Eleven site before construction can begin.

This is essentially a legal and zoning cleanup step so the project can move forward.

## Why This Location

The site is located at the intersection of:

• **Myrick Lane**, which is planned to become a **six-lane arterial** and is currently four lanes  
• **S. Beauchamp Boulevard**, which is a **four-lane arterial**

It is surrounded by:

• homes to the south  
• mini-warehouses to the east  
• vacant industrial land to the north and west

This kind of arterial-to-arterial intersection is commonly targeted for large convenience stores with fuel service.

## What City Staff Is Recommending

City planning staff is recommending approval with several conditions:

• a **20-foot landscaped buffer** on both streets with trees  
• **100 percent real brick and stone exterior**  
• landscape islands in every parking row  
• a **replat must be approved before any building permit is issued**

## What Happens Next

### Tuesday, January 20

The **Planning and Zoning Commission** will hold a public hearing and make a recommendation.

### Monday, February 9

**City Council** will hold the final public hearing and vote.

If approved, the applicant can proceed with the required replat and then apply for building permits.

## Bottom Line

Princeton has already approved a gas-station use at **Myrick and Beauchamp**.

The current request would expand the approved site so that it matches a **7-Eleven-branded plan set**.

In other words, the use itself is already approved. What is now moving through the process is the **site expansion and replat** needed to let the project move toward permits and construction.
