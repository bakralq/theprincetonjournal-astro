## Overview

On **December 8, 2025**, the Princeton City Council approved several actions advancing the city's planned **Public Safety Complex**, including site selection, land control arrangements, architect selection, and authorization of professional design funding.

The approvals were made during a **properly posted public meeting**.

The project is intended to support **long-term police and fire operations in a rapidly growing city**.

---

## Actions Approved

City Council approved the following steps:

• Selected the **Corporate Drive at Myrick Lane site**

• Approved a **land/lease proposal with the Princeton Economic Development Corporation (PEDC)** to control the project site through a long-term lease arrangement

• Approved a **professional architecture and design services contract** with **Brinkley Sargent Wiginton Architects, Inc. (BSW)**

• Authorized **up to $4,243,022** for professional services and reimbursables related to the BSW contract

---

## Purpose of the Public Safety Complex

The proposed complex is intended to:

• Serve as a **centralized facility for Princeton Police Department operations**

• Provide **administrative space for the Princeton Fire Department**

• Improve **coordination, communication, and response capacity**

• Replace **existing facilities that are undersized**

• Prepare the city for **future public safety needs as population grows**

Police Chief **James Waters**, Fire Chief **Shannon Stephens**, and Mayor **Eugene Escobar Jr.** stated that the project supports **long-term public safety readiness and operational capacity**.

---

## Site Selection

City officials evaluated three potential sites.

The selected location:

**Corporate Drive at Myrick Lane**  
Located within the **Princeton Business Park** and owned by the **Princeton Economic Development Corporation (PEDC)**.

Other sites considered:

• **3000 block of Highway 380**, removed from consideration due to the bypass and one-way service road frontage.

• **Beauchamp Road at Monte Carlo**, where land constraints were identified.

---

## Land Control and Lease Structure

The approved land proposal involves approximately **7.40 acres owned by the PEDC**.

Key lease terms summarized from the agenda materials include:

• **Lease term:** 30 years  
• **Annual lease payment:** $67,166.67  
• **Payment deadline:** September 30 each year  
• **Use restriction:** Police and fire public safety purposes only  
• **Construction deadline:** Must begin within **30 months of the lease effective date**

Additional provisions include:

• **Purchase option:** $2,015,000 minus lease payments already made  
• **Option consideration:** $100 non-refundable  
• **Property condition:** Leased **as-is, where-is**  
• **Assignment or subleasing:** Requires **PEDC approval**

## Architecture and Design Contract

The professional services contract with **Brinkley Sargent Wiginton Architects (BSW)** totals **$4,243,022**.

Breakdown:

**Basic Services:**  
$2,920,500  
Based on an estimated **$36 million initial construction budget**.

Phase billing schedule:

• 15 percent  
• 30 percent  
• 30 percent  
• 5 percent  
• 20 percent

**Supplemental Services:**  
Up to **$1,262,522**, billed only for work actually performed.

**Reimbursables:**  
Estimated **$60,000**, billed at cost plus 10 percent, subject to approval.

---

## Current Status

As of **December 8, 2025**, the City Council has approved:

• Site selection  
• Land control structure  
• Architect selection  
• Professional design services scope  
• Initial design funding authorization

No construction contract has been awarded at this stage.

The project will move forward through **design development, planning, and future construction approvals**.
