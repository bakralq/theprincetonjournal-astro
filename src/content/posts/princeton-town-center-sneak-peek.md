## What it is

Princeton Town Center is a planned **100-plus-acre retail district** at the northwest corner of **W. Princeton Drive (U.S. 380) and N. Beauchamp Boulevard**, across from Walmart.

According to the city’s project page, the center is expected to feature **35 or more national brands**, anchored by **Lowe's Home Improvement, Market Street, Texas Roadhouse, and Hobby Lobby**.

City estimates project the development could generate **more than $3 million in annual sales tax revenue** once the center is fully built.

Private leasing and trade materials describe a big-box shopping center of roughly **420,000 to 500,000 square feet**. The range comes from different third-party marketing estimates.

Some marketing previews list potential tenants including **Market Street, Lowe’s, Hobby Lobby, TJ Maxx, and PetSmart**. These names come from promotional marketing materials rather than city permits and should be treated as **preliminary until official filings appear**.

## Who’s developing it

City records list **Trination Global Investments P67 Partnership** as the official zoning applicant and developer.

Some city agendas and trade sources also mention **The Seitz Group** in connection with the project. However, the applicant of record in zoning filings remains **Trination**.

## What’s happening right now

• **Oct. 20, 2025 (Planning & Zoning Commission):** The P&Z Commission held a public hearing on case **ZA20243021**, a zone map amendment covering **66.623 acres** in the **Hardin Wright Survey (A-957)**.

• **Oct. 27, 2025 (City Council at 6:30 p.m.):** City Council is scheduled to hold its public hearing and consider final action on the same zoning case.

## How we got here

• **March 2024:** The first Town Center planned development amendment was noticed for City Council review, covering approximately **91.169 acres**.

• **Aug. 19, 2024:** A **preliminary plat for about 91.6 acres** went before the Planning & Zoning Commission.

• **Aug. 26, 2024:** City Council approved the preliminary plat.

• **March 17, 2025:** The applicant withdrew the earlier zoning case before submitting the current **66.623-acre zone map amendment**, which is now moving forward.

## Tenants: what’s official vs. provisional

• City information still lists **Lowe’s, Market Street, Texas Roadhouse, and Hobby Lobby** as anchor stores along with **35 or more national brands**.

• Private marketing and trade press materials reference potential anchors such as **Kroger Marketplace** and **The Home Depot**, along with names like **PetSmart, T.J. Maxx, and HomeGoods** appearing in some council-packet references.

• City economic-development officials noted on **Oct. 3** that **no leases were yet imminent**.

In short, tenant lists remain **unconfirmed until formal lease announcements or building permits appear**.

## Approvals in Princeton (quick guide)

Large developments typically move through several stages:

**Zoning (PD or ZMA) → Platting → Site Plans and Building Permits → Inspections → Certificates of Occupancy**

Princeton Town Center completed preliminary platting in **2024** and is currently in the **zoning map amendment stage** for the **66.623-acre portion** of the site.

## The U.S. 380 corridor

U.S. 380 is Princeton’s primary growth corridor.

The Town Center site sits at a key commercial node across from Walmart and near several other retail developments, exactly where city planning materials and broker reports show long-term commercial clustering forming along the highway.

## The Quick Read

The **Princeton Town Center** is a planned **100-acre retail development** moving toward final approval at the northwest corner of **U.S. 380 and N. Beauchamp Boulevard**, across from Walmart.

City materials list **Lowe’s, Market Street, Texas Roadhouse, and Hobby Lobby** as anchor tenants and project more than **$3 million in annual sales tax revenue** once the center is complete.

Private marketing materials also reference **Kroger Marketplace, The Home Depot, PetSmart, T.J. Maxx, and HomeGoods**, though none of those tenants are officially confirmed.

The developer of record is **Trination Global Investments P67 Partnership**.

After earlier filings in 2024 covering about **91 acres**, the current zoning case covers **66.6 acres**.

The **Planning & Zoning Commission held its hearing on Oct. 20, 2025**, and **City Council is scheduled to vote on Oct. 27, 2025**.

The project remains in the **zoning map amendment stage** and represents the next major retail expansion along Princeton’s rapidly developing **U.S. 380 corridor**.
