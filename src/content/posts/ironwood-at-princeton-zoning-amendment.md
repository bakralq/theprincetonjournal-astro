On Monday, January 12, City Council will hold a public hearing and vote on a zoning amendment request for the **Ironwood at Princeton** multifamily site at **599 W. Princeton Drive**, near **Tickey Creek and U.S. 380**, east of Walmart.

## What Is Being Requested

The applicant, **599 W Princeton LP (Blom Capital)**, is requesting an amendment to the site’s zoning from **PD 12 to PD 12A** for the **15.157-acre property**.

## What the Amendment Would Change

### 1. Building materials

The amendment would expand what can qualify as **masonry** under the PD standards by allowing additional exterior material options, including certain **cementitious products**.

### 2. Parking

The amendment would replace previously required **garages** with **covered parking carports**.

### 3. Amenities

The amendment would remove the previously listed **dog wash** and **car wash** amenities.

### 4. Gate requirement

The amendment would remove the requirement for a **staffed, manned entrance gate**.

### 5. Unit count

The amendment would update the total number of units to **306**, aligning the project count with the PD language and related project documents.

## Background Residents May Recognize

The project was originally approved in **2017** as a luxury-style multifamily development.

Construction began in **2021**, then stalled in **2023** following disputes that led to city enforcement actions and partially unfinished structures remaining on site.

**Blom Capital** purchased the property in early **2025**, rebranded the project as **Eco-opulent**, stabilized the site, secured new financing, and later obtained a full building permit.

## What City Staff Is Recommending

City staff recommends approval, but with additional quality-related conditions, including:

• at least **two exterior materials** and **two color tones** per building  
• stricter limits on which **cementitious panels** may qualify as masonry  
• stronger landscaping and fully screened parking near homes  
• **decorative metal or powder-coated balcony railings**, rather than chain-link or temporary systems

## Process and Current Status

• Notices were mailed to nearby property owners and published publicly  
• As of the staff report filing, **no public comments had been received**  
• The **Planning and Zoning Commission voted 4 to 0 in favor**  
• City Council may **approve, modify, table, or deny** the request

## Why This Matters

This case affects one of the city’s most visible stalled multifamily sites along the U.S. 380 corridor.

If approved, the amendment could shape how the project is revived, what design standards are applied, and how the final development fits into the surrounding area.
