## Engineering Status as of October 1, 2025

The most recent engineering review letter located for Princeton Town Center is dated **October 1, 2025**.

In that letter, the City’s engineering consultant marked multiple drainage items as:

• Not addressed  
• Partially addressed  
• Still pending  

These were not minor comments. Items involving:

• stormwater outfalls  
• detention pond design  
• erosion protection  
• drainage calculations  
• easement documentation  

were still unresolved at that time.

In plain language, as of October 1, 2025, the drainage system for this project **did not yet have full engineering clearance**.

This is separate from zoning approval. Construction plans require **civil engineering sign off** before permits can move forward.

Before approval, engineers must confirm that:

• runoff will not increase flooding risk  
• detention ponds are properly designed  
• downstream discharge is accounted for  
• legal drainage easements are secured  

According to the October 1 letter, those boxes were still open.

## Public Discussion Earlier in 2025

A former Planning and Zoning commissioner told *The Princeton Journal* he believes drainage concerns raised in early 2025 are connected to the project’s delays.

He states that during a **March 2025 vote on a conveyance plat**, he flagged what he viewed as a major issue:

• a single commercial tract, now the Home Depot lot, could end up handling drainage tied to a much larger portion of the project  
• the proposed stormwater outfall pointed toward private property  
• an easement for that discharge had not yet been secured at the time  
• the design could create downstream liability concerns  

He maintains that this configuration was flawed and contributed to delays.

He also notes that the **October 1, 2025 engineering review letter**, which he obtained through a **Public Information Request**, still showed multiple unresolved drainage comments.

Recordings and agenda materials from the **February and March 2025 meetings** are publicly available.

## What Has Happened Since

City records show that **construction plans were resubmitted for review on October 27, 2025**.

As of **February 18, 2026**, no publicly posted engineering approval letter dated after October 1, 2025 has been identified in the materials reviewed.

This does **not prove approval has not occurred**. It only means that no later public sign off document has been located so far.

## Acreage Context

The October engineering review covers about **66 acres**.

The original zoning for the project covers roughly **96 acres**.

For large developments, downstream drainage modeling often examines **full build out conditions**. When phases are interconnected, drainage review timelines can become more complex.

## Bottom Line

• Drainage engineering comments were still unresolved as of October 1, 2025  
• Drainage responsibility and outfall direction were discussed in public meetings earlier in 2025
