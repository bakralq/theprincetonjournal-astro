## City Council Meeting

**Wednesday, January 28, 2026**

## What Item L8 Is

City Council will hear a presentation from Police Chief **James Waters** on the Princeton Police Department’s 2025 activity.

The presentation covers:

• overall police operations during 2025  
• the department’s required annual racial profiling report under Texas law  

This is an informational update.

There is **no vote** and **no new policy or ordinance** attached to this item.

## Cost to Taxpayers

There is no direct cost tied to this agenda item.

Council is not being asked to approve new spending here.

## Why This Matters Now

Princeton’s population is growing very quickly.

• In 2025, the U.S. Census Bureau identified Princeton as the **fastest-growing city in the country**  
• The city’s population increased by about **30 percent in one year**, from roughly **28,000 to more than 37,000 residents**  
• City projections in the report show an estimated **2026 population of 57,499**

More residents mean more calls for service, more traffic on the roads, and more pressure on officers and support staff.

This report is one of the few public snapshots showing how the department is trying to keep up.

## What Princeton Police Handled in 2025

### Staffing at the end of 2025

• **45 sworn police officers**  
• **4 civilian staff members**

### Calls for service

• **28,346 total calls in 2025**  
• About a **34 percent increase compared to 2024**

### Response times

• Average response time: **6.59 minutes**  
• Average time spent on scene: **20.35 minutes**

### Arrests and crime snapshots

• **626 total arrests in 2025**  
• About a **6.5 percent increase** from the year before

Selected incident totals reported for 2025:

• Theft: **147**  
• Burglary: **64**  
• Motor vehicle theft: **24**  
• Simple assault: **183**  
• Aggravated assault: **50**  
• Robbery: **4**  
• Rape: **23**

### Traffic accidents

• **801 traffic accidents** occurred inside city limits during 2025

## Racial Profiling Report, in Plain Language

Texas law requires every police department to collect and report traffic-stop data once a year.

For Princeton, the 2025 report:

• covers **January 1 through December 31, 2025**  
• explains how traffic-stop information is collected  
• summarizes racial and ethnic data required by statute  
• includes analysis and any recommendations

City Council receives this report as part of its oversight and accountability role.

The report includes detailed tables breaking down traffic stops by race, ethnicity, reason for stop, and outcome.

## Other 2025 Department Highlights

According to the presentation materials, in 2025 the department:

• hired **7 new patrol officers** and **2 school resource officers**  
• promoted **5 sergeants** and **1 lieutenant**  
• launched a **community camera registration program**  
• deployed **Flock Safety license plate reader cameras**  
• logged **6,388 hours of officer training**  
• processed **1,253 public records requests**

## Questions Residents May Want to Ask

If you attend or watch the meeting, questions worth listening for include:

• How does the department plan to keep response times stable as population continues to grow?  
• What is contributing to the high number of traffic accidents?  
• Do the traffic-stop and racial profiling data show any patterns that need attention?  
• What staffing or other resources may be needed over the next few years?
