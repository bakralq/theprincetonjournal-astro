A roughly **1-acre property on Longneck Road** is moving toward a possible zoning change from **residential estate zoning to commercial zoning**. The Planning and Zoning Commission recommended approval, and the final decision now goes to **City Council**.

## Why This Matters

Zoning is the rulebook.

If City Council adopts **PD 46** for this site, the property would be legally positioned for commercial use going forward. The application states the intended use is a **small office building**.

## What the Record Shows People Are Worried About

### Flood and drainage

Staff says the site is adjacent to the **Ticky Creek floodplain**. Small portions of the property are in **FEMA Zone X**, while a smaller portion is in **Zone AE**, the 100-year floodplain.

According to staff, a **flood study will be required before construction**.

A written protest submitted in the case record says the property drains toward neighboring land and is tied to the **Ticky Creek tributary**. The protest argues that additional pavement could push runoff onto nearby property and states that there is **no drainage easement on that neighboring land tied to the proposed development**.

### Traffic and safety

A written protest describes **Longneck Road as unsafe** and points specifically to the **Longneck Road and Monte Carlo area**.

Staff says Longneck Road is planned to become a **four-lane undivided road**. According to staff, the developer must either widen the road to city standards or escrow the cost, and **City Council would have to approve any escrow arrangement**.

### Utilities and infrastructure

Staff says existing **water and sewer lines have adequate capacity** to serve the site.

Even where utility capacity exists, more intense land use can still create additional traffic load and future right-of-way or easement pressure as the surrounding area develops.

### Compatibility and property impacts

The proposed zoning change would place **commercial zoning next to nearby SF-E and agricultural areas**.

The written protest argues that commercial activity, lighting, traffic, noise, and construction are not compatible with the area’s current rural character and could interfere with active agricultural operations. It also raises concerns about **property value impacts and long-term land usability tied to drainage changes**.

### Legal context

A written protest was submitted under **Texas Local Government Code 211.006(d)**, asserting that the statutory protest threshold has been met.

If that protest is validated, final approval by City Council would require a **three-fourths supermajority vote**.

## Commission Action Record

Commissioner **Bakr Al Qaraghuli** made a motion to recommend denial based on:

• drainage and floodplain exposure  
• traffic safety  
• land use compatibility

That motion did **not receive a second**.

The Commission then voted to recommend approval, with Commissioner **Bakr Al Qaraghuli casting the sole vote against approval**.

## Quick Read

A proposed rezoning on Longneck Road would change a roughly **1-acre site from residential estate to commercial use**. Planning and Zoning recommended approval, sending the case to **City Council for final action**.

The record reflects concerns raised through a written protest and staff review involving **floodplain exposure near Ticky Creek, drainage, traffic safety, and compatibility with nearby rural and agricultural uses**.

Staff states that a **flood study will be required**, utilities have capacity, and **road widening or escrow may be required**.

A written protest also claims that a **supermajority vote may be required under state law**.

At Planning and Zoning, Commissioner **Bakr Al Qaraghuli** made a motion to recommend denial that did not receive a second, and the Commission later voted to recommend approval with **one vote against**.

City Council is scheduled to hold a public hearing on **January 12, 2026 at 6:30 PM**.
