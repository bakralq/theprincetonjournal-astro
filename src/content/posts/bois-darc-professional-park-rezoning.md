## What Is Being Proposed

Princeton Economic Development Corporation is requesting a rezoning for approximately **53.3865 acres** at **Bois D’ Arc Professional Park** near **S. Beauchamp Boulevard and Myrick Lane**, by Corporate Drive.

**Case:** ZA20253149

## Current Zoning

**Manufacturing Industrial Light (M-1)**

## Requested Zoning

**Planned Development 47 (PD47)** with base zoning **C-1 commercial (PD47/C-1)**.

According to the city notice, the purpose is to allow **professional office and retail/commercial uses** in place of industrial manufacturing uses, with design standards aimed at higher-quality development.

According to the staff report, the purpose is to shift the site from industrial uses to a **professional business park** that is more compatible with nearby neighborhoods.

## Where It Sits

The site is located:

• Near the signalized intersection of **S. Beauchamp Boulevard and Myrick Lane**  
• South of **U.S. 380** near Walmart  
• Between surrounding residential areas and planned city projects, including the future city recreation center site nearby

## What PD47 Would Allow

The proposed district is designed to function as a **commercial business area**, allowing offices, medical uses, light retail, and neighborhood-serving services, along with other C-1 uses unless specifically limited by the PD standards.

## Examples Listed in the PD Standards

These are examples only, not a complete list.

### Permitted examples

• Grocer  
• Medical and dental labs  
• Pet shop  
• Dry cleaner  
• Radio and TV studio

### Allowed with a Specific Use Permit

• Drive thru fast food  
• Car wash  
• Convenience store with or without gas pumps  
• Childcare center  
• Research lab  
• Heliport  
• Private club  
• Auto storage  
• Paid parking garage  
• Motorcycle sales and service

## What the PD Standards Would Prohibit

Examples listed in the standards include:

• Auto repair  
• Mini warehouse public storage  
• Pawn shop  
• RV sales and service  
• Motel lodging  
• Other higher-intensity or incompatible uses

The standards distinguish between **hotel** and **motel** uses. A hotel may be allowed only with **internal hallway access**, while a motel is not permitted.

## Design and Compatibility Rules Being Proposed

• Maximum building height: **55 feet**  
• No blank building faces; windows required on each side except where codes prohibit  
• Street-facing facades must have at least **20 percent transparency**  
• Building facades must be **100 percent masonry** as defined, with tilt-wall, CMU, or precast limited to a maximum of **75 percent of any facade** and accent materials limited to a maximum of **25 percent**  
• Parking must be screened from the street with buildings or landscaping  
• Parking islands: **1 island per 15 spaces** with a minimum of **200 square feet** and **1 tree**  
• Parking-area trees: **1 tree per 15 spaces**; island trees may count  
• Open space and landscape: **20 percent minimum**, with a possible administrative reduction to **15 percent**  
• Impervious surface cap: **80 percent**, with a possible administrative increase to **85 percent**  
• Loading, refuse, and rooftop equipment must be screened  
• Refuse must be at least **50 feet from residentially zoned property** and cannot be placed between the building front and the street  
• Electronic message board signs would be allowed

## Infrastructure and Traffic Notes in the Staff Report

According to the staff report:

• Water and sewer are already in the adjacent public streets, and staff says capacity is adequate  
• City drainage for the larger **109-acre area** includes **3 retention ponds**  
• Right-of-way has already been dedicated on key roads, but future developers may still be required to add turn lanes, deceleration lanes, or median modifications

## Why This Matters

This proposal would shift more than 53 acres from light industrial zoning toward a commercial and professional business park model.

That could affect:

• The type of jobs created in this part of Princeton  
• The compatibility of future development with nearby neighborhoods  
• Traffic and infrastructure demands along Beauchamp, Myrick, and nearby corridors  
• Long-term land use around the southern U.S. 380 growth area

For a fast-growing city, zoning decisions like this shape not just what gets built next, but what kind of economic base Princeton builds over time.
